A lot of products say "made in Thailand" on the label. Far fewer can tell you the actual story behind it: the place, the people, the choices, and the reasons. We think that story matters, especially for something as rooted in tradition as a Thai herbal inhaler. So here it is, honestly: where Tom Yam Yadom comes from, why we make it in Koh Samui, and how we think about what goes inside.

## Why Koh Samui

Koh Samui is an island off the eastern coast of southern Thailand, known for its beaches, its coconut groves, and a pace of life that runs on island time. It's also home, which is the simplest reason we make our inhalers here. We didn't pick a manufacturing hub on a spreadsheet. We build where we live, surrounded by the culture that gave the world yadom in the first place.

That proximity isn't sentimental fluff. Making our inhalers here means we're close to the tradition we're drawing on, close to the botanical character of the region, and able to keep the whole process small, hands-on, and under our own eyes rather than handed off to a distant factory.

## Built on a Real Tradition

Tom Yam Yadom didn't appear out of nowhere as a wellness trend. It's a modern, premium take on the traditional Thai yadom, an everyday object that's been part of Thai life for generations (we tell that whole story in [what is yadom](/blog/what-is-yadom-thai-herbal-inhaler)). We grew up with these inhalers. They're not exotic to us. They're ordinary, in the best possible way.

Our founder is a professional Muay Thai fighter, which ties the brand to combat-sports culture authentically rather than as a marketing angle. The discipline, the rituals, the respect for tradition that run through Muay Thai are the same values we wanted in the product. When we talk about focus before a fight or grounding after hard training, that's lived experience, not copywriting.

## The Case for Organic Botanicals

Here's where we make a deliberate choice that costs more and takes more effort: we build around organic botanicals and quality essential oils rather than synthetic fragrance.

### Why Synthetic Is the Easy Road

Most mass-market inhalers lean heavily on synthetic menthol and lab-made fragrance. It's cheaper, it's consistent, and it delivers a sharp hit. But it's also flat and one-dimensional, and it gets harsh and tiresome when you use it repeatedly. For a product meant to be a daily companion, breath after breath, that's a real limitation. We dig into this contrast in our [comparison with Vicks and Boom Boom](/blog/thai-herbal-inhaler-vs-vicks-boom-boom).

### Why We Take the Harder Road

Whole botanicals and real essential oils give a rounder, more layered scent, the kind you can return to again and again without fatigue. It's the difference between a single loud note and an actual chord. For people who care what they're breathing, the wellness crowd, the biohackers, anyone reading ingredient lists, natural simply matters. And honestly, it's the version we want to use ourselves.

This is also where ingredients like borneol come in, the prized traditional aromatic we wrote about in [borneol, the ancient focus ingredient](/blog/borneol-ancient-focus-ingredient), which gives a clarity you don't get from a synthetic shortcut.

## Handcrafted, Not Mass-Produced

Our inhalers are handcrafted, and we mean that literally. Small-batch, hands-on, made by people who care how each one turns out, rather than churned off an anonymous line by the thousand.

That choice has trade-offs we accept on purpose. It's slower and it doesn't scale as effortlessly as a fully automated process. But it lets us hold a standard, keep the craft connected to the people making it, and treat each inhaler as something we'd be happy to hand to a friend. For a premium product rooted in tradition, that's the whole point.
